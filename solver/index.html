Solver¶

SCS is derived as Douglas-Rachford splitting applied to a homogeneous embedding of the quadratic cone program. The high level algorithm is as follows, from an initial \(w^0\) for \(k=0,1,\ldots\) do

\[\begin{split}\begin{align} \tilde u^{k+1} &= (I + \mathcal{Q})^{-1} w^k\\ u^{k+1} &= \Pi_{\mathcal{C}_+}(2\tilde u^{k+1} - w^k)\\ w^{k+1} &= w^k + u^{k+1} - \tilde u^{k+1} \end{align}\end{split}\]

and \(u^k \rightarrow u^\star\) from which we can recover the optimal solution or the certificate of infeasibility. The algorithm consists of three steps. The first step involves solving a linear system of equations:

\[\begin{split}\begin{bmatrix} I + P & A^T \\ A & -I \end{bmatrix}\begin{bmatrix} x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \mu_x \\ -\mu_y \end{bmatrix},\end{split}\]

The second step is the Euclidean projection onto a convex cone, ie,

\[\begin{split}\begin{array}{ll} \mbox{minimize} & \| z - z_0\|_2^2 \\ \mbox{subject to } & z \in \mathcal{K} \end{array}\end{split}\]

Most cone projection operators have relatively simple projection operators.
